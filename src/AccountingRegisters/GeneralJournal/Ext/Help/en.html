When you post a document, AccountingSuite automatically generates general journal entries based on the transactions listed in the document. It assigns general journal entries to accounts using the following procedure:

  1. For items, the inventory/expense, income, and COGS reclass account from the item properties.
  2. The bank, A/R, and A/P accounts are taken from the document being posted.
  3. Other accounts are taken from the Posting accounts tab in Accounting settings.
     

You cannot edit or delete entries that are generated automatically. However, you can use the General journal entry document for adding entries that are not based on documents, or for correcting general journal data. 

Each general journal entry line includes the following columns:

  • Entry type. The journal entry type: debit or credit.
  • Recorder. The document that generated the journal entry.
  • Period. The day when the journal entry was created.
  • Currency. The transaction currency.

    This field is only available if the Multicurrency check box is selected in Accounting settings. This field is only filled for the following account types that support currency amount tracking: Bank, Accounts receivable, and Accounts payable.
  • Memo. Notes taken from the document that generated the journal entry.
  • Account. The account code.
  • Acc. description. The account description.
  • Amount FCY. The transaction amount in the foreign currency.

    This field is only filled if the Multicurrency check box is selected in Accounting settings, and only for entries with bank, A/P, and A/R accounts.
  • Amount. The transaction amount in the reporting currency.
     

The following list describes transactions that are automatically generated for specific documents.

Bank reconciliation

    Interest earned:

  • Debit—bank account
  • Credit—bank interest earned account

    Bank service charge:
     
  • Debit—bank service charge account
  • Credit—bank account
     

Cash purchase

  • Debit—inventory account (for inventory items) or expense account (for noninventory items)
  • Credit—bank account
  • Debit—tax payable account (if the VAT check box is selected in Accounting settings)
     

Cash receipt

    If the Cash receipt is based on Sales invoice:
  • Credit—A/R account
  • Debit—bank account (for payments deposited by the cash receipt) or undeposited funds account (for payments deposited later)

    If the Cash receipt is based on Purchase return:
     
  • Credit—A/P account
  • Debit—bank account (for payments deposited by the cash receipt) or undeposited funds account (for payments deposited later)

    FX gain:
     
  • Credit—exchange gain account

    FX loss:
     
  • Debit—exchange loss account
     

Cash sale

  • Debit—bank account
  • Credit—income account
  • Debit—COGS (for inventory items)
  • Credit—inventory account (for inventory items)
  • Credit—tax payable account (if the Sales tax (US) check box or VAT check box is selected in Accounting settings) 
      

Check

    Transactions are only posted if the check was entered manually. Transactions are not posted for checks based on Cash purchase or Invoice payment documents.
     
  • Debit—accounts selected in line items
  • Credit—bank account
     

Deposit

  • Debit—bank account
  • Credit—undeposited funds account
     

Invoice payment

    If the Invoice payment is based on Purchase invoice:

  • Debit—A/P account
  • Credit—bank account

    If the Invoice payment is based on Sales return:
     
  • Debit—A/R account
  • Credit—bank account

    FX gain:
     
  • Credit—exchange gain account

    FX loss:
     
  • Debit—exchange loss account
     

Purchase invoice

    If the Purchase invoice describes a purchase transaction:

  • Debit—inventory account (for inventory items) or expense account (for noninventory items)
  • Credit—A/P account
  • Debit—tax payable account (if the VAT check box is selected in Accounting settings)

    If the Purchase invoice is used for entering initial balance: 
     
  • Credit—A/P account
     

Purchase return

    If the Purchase return describes a sales return transaction: 

  • Debit—A/P account
  • Credit—inventory account (for inventory items) or expense account (for noninventory items)
  • Credit—tax payable account (if the VAT check box is selected in Accounting settings)

    If the Purchase return is used for entering initial balance:
     
  • Debit—A/P account
     

Sales invoice

    If the Sales invoice describes a sales transaction: 

  • Debit—A/R account
  • Credit—income account
  • Debit—COGS (for inventory items)
  • Credit—inventory account (for inventory items)
  • Credit—tax payable account (if the Sales tax (US) check box or VAT check box is selected in Accounting settings) 

    If the Sales invoice is used for entering initial balance: 
     
  • Debit—A/R account 
      

Sales return

    If the Sales return describes a sales return transaction: 

  • Debit—income account
  • Credit—A/R account
  • Debit—inventory account (for inventory items)
  • Credit—COGS (for inventory items)
  • Debit—tax payable account (if the Sales tax (US) check box or VAT check box is selected in Accounting settings) 

    If the Sales return is used for entering initial balance:
     
  • Credit—A/R account
