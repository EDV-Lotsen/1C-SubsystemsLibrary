The cash receipt is a document used to record Sales invoice and Purchase return incoming payments. You have the option to use credit memos for payments.

Cash receipts generate general journal transactions. For detailed information about transactions that are generated when a cash receipt is posted, see the General journal help section.

To enter a cash receipt

  1. In the Bank section, click Cash receipts and then click Create.
  2. Enter or select the following:

    • Number. By default the system assigns consecutive numbers when documents are saved, and no user input is required.

      You have the option to enter a custom number instead. Document numbers can include prefixes. For example, if you specify the number "CR-1", your next cash receipts are automatically numbered "CR-2", "CR-3", and so on.  
    • Deposit type. Select one of the following:
      • Undeposited funds. The payment is deposited later individually or grouped with other payments using the Deposit document.
      • Account. The payment is deposited by this document.
    • Bank account. The account (e.g. bank, cash, credit card, PayPal, etc.) where the payment is deposited. 
    • Date. The cash receipt date.

      This field is prefilled with the current date.
    • Ref #. Optional transaction number (for example, payment ID/number).
    • Cash payment. The paid amount.
    • Company. The company that is paying you. 
    • Company code. The company code (read-only).

      This field is only available if the Display company codes check box is selected in Accounting settings. 
    • Payment method. The payment method.

      After you select a company, the system will display all unpaid sales invoices and purchase returns for the company in line items of the Documents list, and available credit memos in the Credit memos list.
  3. Enter the payment amounts.
    • In the Credit memos list, enter the amounts that are being paid from credit memos.
    • In the Documents list, enter the amounts that are being paid against each unpaid document.
  4. In the Memo field, you can enter notes on the cash receipt.
  5. Click Post and close.

    The informational (read-only) section at the bottom of the form contains the following data:
    • Total FCY. The total amount in the transaction currency.

      This field is only available if the Multicurrency check box is selected in Accounting settings. 
    • Total. The total amount in the reporting currency.
