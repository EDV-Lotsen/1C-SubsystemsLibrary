A sales invoice records a customer's liability to you and income from the transaction. It calculates cost of goods sold (COGS) and updates inventory balances. It also can be used for entering beginning balances.

Sales invoices generate general journal transactions. For detailed information about transactions that are generated when a sales invoice is posted, see the General journal help section.

You have the option to generate a sales invoice document from one or several Sales Order documents. In this case most of the sales invoice fields are automatically filled from the sales orders.

To enter a sales invoice

  1. In the Sales section, click Sales invoices and then click Create.

    -OR-

    In a Sales Order document, click Generate and then click Sales invoice.
  2. Enter or select the following:
    • Number. By default the system assigns consecutive numbers when documents are saved, and no user input is required.

      You have the option to enter a custom number instead. Document numbers can include prefixes. For example, if you specify the number "SI-1", your next sales invoices are automatically numbered "SI-2", "SI-3", and so on. 
    • Ref #. Optional additional transaction number.
    • Currency. The transaction currency.

      This field is only available if the Multicurrency check box is selected in Accounting settings.
    • Terms. The payment term.
    • Price includes VAT. Indicates whether the prices specified in the sales invoice include VAT.

      This field is only available if the VAT check box is selected in Accounting settings. 
    • Date. The sales invoice date.

      This field is prefilled with the current date.
    • Exchange rate (shown as currency symbols, for example, $/€). The exchange rate between the transaction currency and the reporting currency.

      The system retrieves the exchange rate from the Exchange rates list.

      If you change the sales invoice date, the system recalculates the exchange rate. If you change the date or currency, the system does not recalculate prices in the line items, therefore it is recommended that you specify the date and currency before entering the line items.

      This field is only available if the Multicurrency check box is selected in Accounting settings.  
    • Due date. The payment due date.

      This field is calculated automatically based on the document date and the Terms field.
    • A/R account. The accounts receivable account used for G/L posting.
    • Ship to. The shipping address.

      This field is prefilled with the customer's default shipping address.
    • Customer. The customer (here you can only select companies of the customer type).

      Once you select a customer, a list of open and backordered sales orders for this customer is displayed. Select the sales orders you want to fulfill with this sales invoice.

      The field name can be customized (for example, to Client) in the Customer name field in Accounting settings. 
    • Company code. The company code (read-only).

      This field is only available if the Display company codes check box is selected in Accounting settings.
    • Location. The location of the warehouse where the sold items are stored.

      This field is only available if the Multilocation check box is selected in Accounting settings. 
    • Sales order. The sales order that the sales invoice is based on.
  3. On the Line items tab, you can edit the list of items, and specify their prices and quantities.

    If the sales invoice is created based on one or more sales orders, items are prefilled from the selected sales orders. Item prices are prefilled from the price list. The system automatically calculates a line total for each item.

    If the VAT check box is selected in Accounting settings, a default VAT code is prefilled for each item.

    If the Sales tax (US) check box is selected in Accounting settings, the default sales tax type is prefilled for each item (by default, inventory items are taxable and noninventory items are nontaxable).
  4. Enter or select the following:
    • Memo. In this field you can enter notes on the sales invoice.
    • Beginning balance. Indicates whether the sales invoice is used for entering beginning balances.

      This field is only available if the Beg. balances in documents check box is selected in Accounting settings.
  5. Click Post and close.

    The informational (read-only) section at the bottom of the form contains the following data:
    • Sales tax. The sales tax based on the sales tax code assigned to the customer company and the sales tax types assigned to line items.
    • VAT. VAT calculated based on the VAT codes assigned to items.

      When you post the sales invoice, a VAT liability is recorded to the General journal.

      This field is only available if the VAT check box is selected in Accounting settings.
    • Total FCY. The total amount in the transaction currency.

      This field is only available if the Multicurrency check box is selected in Accounting settings. 
    • Total. The total amount in the reporting currency.
