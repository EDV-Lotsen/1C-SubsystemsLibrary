In order to administrate scheduled and background jobs the application supports following functionality:

  1. Scheduled jobs execution monitor.
  2. Scheduled jobs.
  3. Background jobs.

 

1. Scheduled job execution monitor

The monitor view can be simple or detailed.

Simple view - includes analysis of the State and End date columns on the Scheduled jobs page; it also includes analysis of the Messages to user and Error details fields on the Scheduled job edit form.

Detailed view - includes analysis of background jobs on the Background jobs tab that were created and executed to execute scheduled job.

Detailed view displays background jobs filtered by a specific scheduled job and analysis of background job attributes. Note, that scheduled job execution details are obtained by selection of a part of details of the last background job execution.

Note: the filter to background jobs is applied only after clicking the Refresh button, so after changing filter settings one should execute this command.

2. Scheduled jobs

Button Details
Refresh Refreshes the list of scheduled jobs (the filtered list of background jobs will also be refreshed, as both lists are interdependent).
Add Opens a form for creating a new scheduled job.
Change Opens a form for editing a scheduled job.

Copy

 Opens a form for creating a new scheduled job filled with values from the copied scheduled job.

Delete

 Deletes a scheduled job selected in the list, except for predefined scheduled jobs.
Schedule Opens a form for editing a schedule of a scheduled job.
Run now

Executes the handler of the scheduled job in the current session (for file mode) or in a background job on server (for client/server mode). When a handler is executed in a background job on server, the last execution date, which is used for execution on schedule, is not changed.

 

3. Background jobs

Filter Details
By state

Allows to filter by a state (this complements filtering by period and filtering by scheduled job):
1) Completed,
2) Completed with errors,
3) Canceled by user,
4) Executing.

By period

Allows to filter by a period (this complements filtering by state and filtering by scheduled job):
1) Custom - allows to specify a custom period,
2) Without filter - no filter by period will be applied,
3) Night - standard period of 12 hours starting at 9:00 PM till 09:00 AM,
4) Yesterday - standard period of 24 hours starting at 12:00 AM till 11:59 PM of yesterday,
5) Today - standard period of 24 hours starting at 12:00 AM till 11:59 PM of today.

By scheduled job Allows to filter by a scheduled job (this complements filtering by period and filtering by state):
1) The check box enables or disables this filter,
2) The scheduled job with the <not defined> name is used to filter background jobs, started for execution of other (not scheduled) jobs.
Button Details
Refresh Refreshes the filtered list of background jobs (the list of scheduled jobs will also be refreshed, as both lists are interdependent).
Open Opens a form with background job details.
Cancel Cancels execution of a selected background job (if it is executed currently).
