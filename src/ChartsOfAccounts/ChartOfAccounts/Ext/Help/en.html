AccountingSuite includes a basic chart of accounts that you can customize for your needs.

Each account is assigned one of the predefined account types:

  • Accounts payable (typical balance: credit)
  • Accounts receivable (typical balance: debit)
  • Accumulated depreciation (typical balance: debit)
  • Bank (typical balance: debit)
  • Cost of sales (typical balance: debit)
  • Equity (typical balance: credit)
  • Expense (typical balance: debit)
  • Fixed asset (typical balance: debit)
  • Income (typical balance: credit)
  • Inventory (typical balance: debit)
  • Long term liability (typical balance: debit)
  • Other current asset (typical balance: debit)
  • Other current liability (typical balance: credit)
  • Other expense (typical balance: debit)
  • Other income (typical balance: credit)
  • Other noncurrent asset (typical balance: debit)
      

To add an account

  1. In Accounting section, click Chart of accounts and then click Create.
  2. Enter or select the following:
    • Code. A unique account identifier.

      The system creates it for you automatically but you can change it.
    • Description. The account name as it is displayed in lists and documents.
    • Account type. The account type that is selected from the list of predefined types.
    • Currency. Account currency.

      The system only allows to enter currency for the following account types that support currency amount tracking: Bank, Accounts receivable, and Accounts payable.

      This field is only available if the Multicurrency check box is selected in Accounting settings. 
    • 1099 category. The 1099 category of the account.

      You can assign the same category to multiple accounts.

      This field is only available if the Sales tax (US) check box is selected in Accounting settings.
    • Cash flow section. The cash flow report section where the account cash flow is displayed.
    • Noncash income/expense. Indicates whether the account is used for tracking noncash income or expense for the purposes of generating the cash flow statement.

      The most typical use case is for fixed asset depreciation expense accounts.
    • Other comprehensive income. Indicates whether the account transactions are not included in the main income statement report, but instead are included in the other comprehensive income report.
    • Income tax account. Indicates whether the account transactions placed in the "Income tax" section of the income statement report. 
    • Memo. Your notes on the account.
  3. Click Write and close.
