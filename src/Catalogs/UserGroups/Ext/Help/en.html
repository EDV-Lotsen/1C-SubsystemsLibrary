This list represents user groups.

User groups can be used for groupping of large number of users and also userful when it is more convenient to specify not single users but a bulk of users, for example, when setting access for business process  task performers.

Groups can be viewed as a flat list or hierarchically. In a hierarchical list users of subordinate groups are implicitly included in container groups.

Groups can be created according to the company structure, for example:

  • Company
    • Management
      • Division 1
      • Division 2
    • Management heads
    • Engineering and technical service
      • Division 1
      • Division 2
        • Team 1
        • Team 2
      • Engineering service managers
    • Itemion
      • Workshop 1
        • Section 1
        • Section 2
      • Workshop 2
        • Section 1
        • Section 2
        • Section 3
      • Itemion managers
    • Company heads
    • Inventory team

 

There is All users predefined group, that includes all users implicitly.
